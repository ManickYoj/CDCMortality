Why do 8th Grade Graduates Live Longer?

Each year, the CDC publishes a dataset of deaths from that year. Approximately 2.5 million people die in the US each year and each one gets a row in the dataset with a slew of demographic data. Below , I have grouped all deaths from 2003, 2008, and 2013 by their highest educational attainment. The vertical axis represents the average age of death for each group. From this data, a question arises: Why do 8th grade graduates live longer than their more educated fellows? Note: Those who died with no formal education are excluded because they have a drastically lower average life expectancy due to those who die from infant-specific diseases.

Why do those who only reach an elementary level of education live longer than their more educated peers? Are they dying of something different? To answer that question, the below visualization shows the cause of death by educational attainment. If those whose highest educational attainment was elementary school died of something different, the circles, representing the percentage of an educational group that died of a particular cause, should change in size from row to row. Mouse over the circles for exact percentages, and use the controls if you want to drill down further into the data.
